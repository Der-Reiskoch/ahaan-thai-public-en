Ahaan Thai Glossary

Terms and Concepts from Thai Culinary Culture

This glossary collects important terms from Thai cuisine with their Thai script and a brief explanation.
TransliterationThaiExplanation
Ahaan DjeReligiously motivated diet from Chinese Buddhist tradition. No meat, animal products, or strongly scented plants such as garlic and onions. Mostly fully vegan, especially prevalent during the Vegetarian Festival or “Tesagan Gin Dje” ( ).
Ahaan MangsawiratVegetarian food in the western sense. Meat and seafood are omitted, but eggs may be included. Fish sauce and oyster sauce are often still used in Thailand.
Ahaan Tam SangMade-to-order dish, typical of Thai street kitchens and wok stalls. Guests choose the dish and protein, which is then cooked fresh in the wok.
Khao GaengStreet stall or canteen serving pre-cooked curries and dishes displayed in trays. Guests choose one or more dishes, which are served over rice.
Krueang GaengSpice paste used as the aromatic base for Thai curries, traditionally pounded in a mortar from fresh ingredients such as chilis, galangal, lemongrass, garlic, and shallots.
Nam PrikUmbrella term for the diverse range of Thai chili dips and pastes served as a condiment or side dish. Consistency and heat level vary considerably by region and recipe.
